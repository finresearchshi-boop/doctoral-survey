Doctoral Experience Survey

Thank you for taking part. This survey explores doctoral experiences, support, supervision, wellbeing, success factors, funding, mobility, conference participation, and post-PhD plans. Responses are collected without names or email addresses; please avoid including identifying details in free-text answers if you want to stay anonymous.

Required Sections

Background And Core Questions

All background questions and the first four main survey questions are required. The remaining reflective questions are optional.

Two-Part Format

Page 1 covers concise background information. Page 2 moves into the longer reflective questions so the survey feels lighter to begin with.

Privacy Note

Responses are routed to Google Forms, but the survey does not ask for names or email addresses.

Configuration needed

Main Survey Questions

Questions 9-12 are required. Questions 13-18 are optional.

9* What kinds of support were most important to you during your PhD?

You may wish to reflect on academic guidance, emotional encouragement, feedback on writing, methods or statistics support, peer support, and funding or administrative support.

You may wish to consider frequency of meetings, clarity of expectations, style of feedback, availability, encouragement versus independence, and responsiveness.

You may wish to reflect on the beginning stage, the middle stage, the final writing stage, and when you needed more guidance versus more independence.

You may wish to consider research training, workshops, cohort activities, funding opportunities, administrative efficiency, writing groups, mentoring, and access to library, data, or software resources.

You may wish to reflect on whether you felt connected or isolated, opportunities to present work, informal peer networks, belonging in the department, and intellectual community.

You may wish to consider motivation, time management, writing confidence, uncertainty, methodology, supervision issues, isolation, work-life balance, and family or career pressures.

You may wish to reflect on periods of stress or burnout, what helped maintain motivation, boundaries between work and life, and whether your institution or supervisor offered useful support.

You may wish to reflect on personal qualities, supervisor practices, institutional culture, peer community, resources, and timing or life circumstances.

You may wish to reflect on learning processes, research support, writing, feedback, independence, supervisory relationships, and how expectations may be shifting.
